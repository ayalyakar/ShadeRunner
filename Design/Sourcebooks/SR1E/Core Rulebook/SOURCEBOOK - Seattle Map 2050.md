## Why this note exists separately

Per ingestion policy, book front matter is skipped. The Seattle map is the
one exception — it is a piece of canonical world-state (city layout,
Renraku Arcology placement, named establishments) that will be referenced
by every downstream design section touching Seattle geography, points of
interest, or canon establishments of the 2050s.

## Raw visual description

The map is a pale-blue, top-down schematic of downtown Seattle and
surrounding districts.

- **Compass:** North arrow in the upper-left corner.
- **Title:** "SEATTLE" in a stylized script banner, upper right, accompanied
  by the Shadowrun sun-face totem sigil.
- **Renraku Arcology:** A large rectangular footprint is labeled "RENRAKU
  ARCOLOGY" in the mid-south portion of the map, set back from the
  waterfront. It is visually the single largest structure on the map,
  confirming its canon status as a landmark megastructure.
- **Waterfront:** The south/east edge of the map is a stylized skyline
  silhouette against open water (Puget Sound / Elliott Bay).
- **Street grid:** A dense downtown grid dominates the map. Numbered dots
  across the grid correspond to numbered legend entries (below).
- **Legend:** A blue panel in the lower-right corner, titled "Map Legend",
  subdivided into the categories: **Medical**, **Body Shops**, **Hotels**,
  **Restaurants & Bars**, **Tourist Attractions**. Each category lists
  numbered entries that key to dots on the map.

## Paraphrased summary

Seattle in 2050 is a sprawling metroplex centered on a recognizable
downtown grid, dominated visually and politically by the **Renraku
Arcology** — a corporate megastructure whose footprint exceeds any natural
city block. The map frames the city as a tourist-facing guide (points of
interest, hotels, body shops, medical, restaurants) rather than a runner's
operational chart, but the locations double as:

- Potential job sites (hospitals for bioware extraction, hotels for
  meets).
- Potential contact hangouts (restaurants, bars).
- Landmark navigation anchors (Aquarium, Pacific Science Center).
- Cyberware supply chain nodes (body shops = clinics).

## Design annotations

- **ShadeRunner should treat this map as a scaffold, not a constraint.**
  The simulation will need a finer-grained Seattle (street-level, parcel-
  level for runs) but the landmark placements — especially Renraku
  Arcology, the waterfront, and the hospital cluster — are canon and
  should be preserved.
- **Renraku Arcology is the single most important geographic fixture on
  this map.** Its fate (the 2059 Arcology Shutdown, SR2E / Renraku
  Arcology: Shutdown sourcebook) is era-critical. In timeline-aware canon,
  the Arcology stands and operates normally in SR1E-era play; it becomes
  sealed and hostile in SR2E+ play. Downstream design must handle this
  explicitly.
- **The numbered points-of-interest convention is a good template** for
  ShadeRunner's city-data model: each location has a category, a
  coordinate, a name, and (in the sim) a set of attached services and
  schedule. The map's categories (Medical / Body Shops / Hotels /
  Restaurants & Bars / Tourist Attractions) are a reasonable minimum
  taxonomy to seed from, though ShadeRunner will need many more
  (corporate facilities, police, smuggling drops, fences, stuffer shacks,
  talismongers, decker hangouts, etc.).
- **The legend is tourist-skewed.** No police, no corporate HQs, no
  Lone Star precincts, no shadow establishments. Those data points will
  come from later sections of the book (THE NORTHWEST IN 2050, CONTACTS)
  and later sourcebooks (especially the Seattle Sourcebook). Keep this
  note scoped to the map as printed.
- **Flag for future cross-reference:** when the `SR1E/Core Rulebook/
  SOURCEBOOK - Seattle in the Shadows.md` note gets ingested (entry 297
  in the progress table), re-review this map alongside it to link
  tourist-visible locations with their shadow-side counterparts.
