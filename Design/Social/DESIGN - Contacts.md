# DESIGN - Contacts

## Decision

**DECIDED:** Contacts are a deep relationship system with all four axes active:

1. **Loyalty & Connection** (tabletop-standard).
2. **Contacts have their own agendas / lives** — they pursue goals, can die, betray, retire independent of the player.
3. **Trust history remembered** — missed payments, protection offered, selling out, all permanently shape the relationship.
4. **Romance / intimate relationships** — deep personal bonds, lovers, long-term partners, kids, divorces.

Contacts are how the solo PC accesses specialties the PC doesn't have (decker, mage, rigger, face) and how legwork information flows.

## Connection & Loyalty

- **Connection** (1–6): reach / network size. A Connection-6 contact knows everyone.
- **Loyalty** (1–6): how much they'll risk for the PC.
- Cross-multiplied for many resolutions (favor checks, legwork dice pools, trust thresholds).

## Contact data

Per contact:
- Identity, demographics, district, typical haunt, schedule.
- Profession / specialty (fixer, decker, street doc, corp scion, gang captain, magical tutor, talismonger, etc.).
- Connection and Loyalty to the PC.
- Trust history — notable events.
- Their own agendas and goals (they're sim actors, not static).
- Their own social graph — contacts-of-contacts.
- Current mood / conditions.
- Services they offer and current availability.

## Favors, debts, and currency

- Many interactions resolve in **favors** instead of nuyen: a better deal now for a return call later.
- Debts are tracked explicitly; calling them in is a mechanic.
- A pile of outstanding debts from the PC reduces future favor availability (running dry).

## Hiring specialists (the solo-PC fallback)

When the PC needs a specialist skill (decker for a Matrix dive, mage for a ritual, rigger for drone support):
- Check contact list for relevant specialists.
- Connection + Loyalty + Nuyen + Heat all factor into availability.
- Specialist NPC shows up and acts during the run (combat & narrative).
- Pay / favor cost negotiated up-front; better Loyalty = better terms.
- Specialist can still be killed, captured, flip sides, or fail — part of the risk.

## Romance / intimate bonds

- Available with appropriate contacts / new relationships, regardless of metatype.
- Require time investment; built over sessions.
- Produce strong mechanical bonds (emergency assistance, shared safehouse, wealth pooling).
- Can rupture — trauma mechanic on break-ups, death of partner, betrayal.
- Children are possible and tracked; parenting is an optional long-arc track (see `Character/DESIGN - Downtime.md`).

## Contact acquisition

- Start with chargen-purchased contacts.
- New contacts form naturally via:
  - Introduction from existing contacts (warm lead).
  - Runs (you work with someone; they become a contact).
  - Daily-life interactions (frequent barkeep, neighbor, family).
  - Faction / organization membership.
  - Random encounters that stick.

## Contact loss

- Contacts can die (run casualty, canon event, age, random sim misfortune).
- Contacts can retire, move, vanish.
- Contacts can betray, blacklist the PC, or be burned by PC actions.
- Lost contacts stay lost unless a narrative specifically restores them.

## Display / UX

- Contact roster: groupable (by specialty, district, faction), filterable, annotatable.
- Per-contact page: stats, history, relationship notes, last contact, pending favors, their rumored news.
- Commlink / phone UI for in-world messaging.

## Open questions

- Social graph density — how many contacts is practical / fun for a solo player to track?
- Should contact-of-contact introductions be automatic or explicit favor-spends?
- Family as contacts — how is a "parent" contact handled mechanically?
- Do contacts age and die on metatype lifespans strictly?
