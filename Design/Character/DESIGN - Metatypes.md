# DESIGN - Metatypes

## Decision

**DECIDED:** Only the **Core 5 metatypes** are playable at character creation:

1. **Human**
2. **Elf**
3. **Dwarf**
4. **Ork**
5. **Troll**

## Excluded (at chargen)

- **Metavariants** (nightones, dryads, menehune, oni, gnomes, satyrs, minotaurs, giants, etc.) — not at creation. May appear as NPCs.
- **SURGE / Changelings** — not at creation. Canonical SURGE events can happen *to* an existing PC via event (deferred mechanic).
- **Infected (HMHVV)** — not at creation. May be a post-launch content track (extreme option, huge social/mechanical cost) but deferred.

## Rationale

- Keeps chargen, balance, asset burden, and narrative framing tractable for a solo dev.
- Core 5 covers the vast majority of Shadowrun's design intent.
- Metavariants / SURGE / Infected can be added later as content drops without breaking the baseline.

## Metatype mechanics (to be fleshed out in `Character/DESIGN - Creation.md`)

Each metatype has:
- Attribute min/max adjustments per SR rules (hybrid across editions).
- Racial qualities (low-light / thermographic vision, dermal deposits, etc.).
- Social modifiers (Troll biases, Elf privilege in certain districts/factions).
- Lifespan differences (see `Character/DESIGN - Aging.md`).

## Appearance

Each metatype has broad appearance customization within metatype bounds. Portraits and in-game representation follow the painted 2.5D direction (see `Presentation/DESIGN - Art Direction.md`).

## Open questions

- Exact attribute caps per metatype (which edition's tables to follow).
- Whether goblinization events (latent ork/troll expression) can fire on a Human PC as a narrative event.
- SURGE-on-player as a consequence of high magical exposure — fun but scope.
- Ghouls/HMHVV as a "game over into new game" option vs. real playable mode.
