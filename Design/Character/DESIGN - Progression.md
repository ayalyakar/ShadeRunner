# DESIGN - Progression

## Decision

**DECIDED:** Two complementary progression tracks:

1. **Karma** — classic SR spend-to-improve. Big advances, initiation, qualities.
2. **Skill-use progression** — using a skill nudges it over time (Elder Scrolls / Kingdom Come style).

Karma remains the **authoritative** progression currency. Skill-use grants small bonuses but cannot fully replace karma spends above a threshold.

## Karma

- Awarded for runs (good pay / hard runs / clean execution / style).
- Awarded for downtime milestones (finished training course, completed initiation ordeal).
- Spent on attributes, skills, qualities, initiation grades, specialization, new spells, etc.
- Costs follow hybrid SR scaling: karma cost scales with the *new* rating.

## Skill-use progression

- Each skill has an **XP bar** hidden alongside its rating.
- Using the skill in challenging situations fills the bar (trivial successes give nothing).
- Filling the bar grants a small temporary bonus, or advances a "near ready" flag.
- When near-ready, spending a reduced karma cost promotes the skill fully.
- Rare "aha!" events grant big jumps (after a tense stealth run, after a magical breakthrough).

Skill-use progression models realism without letting the player grind a single skill infinitely.

## Training time in downtime

- Karma spends above a threshold require **training weeks** in-fiction.
- Some advances require a teacher, library, or location (initiation ordeals, cyberdeck tuning, new spell formulae).
- The clock advances during training; the sim continues around the player.

## Milestones / story unlocks

- Some progressions are gated by story and contact trust, not just karma:
  - Initiation grades require a magical group or mentor trust.
  - High-grade cyberware access requires fixer Connection tier.
  - Signature spells / programs from story arcs.
- This knits progression into the social and narrative systems.

## Open questions

- Exact karma-per-run calibration.
- Skill-use progression rate tuning — easy to over- or under-reward.
- Training time defaults — how long does "Improve Firearms from 5 → 6" actually take?
- Respec? Probably no; progression is history.
