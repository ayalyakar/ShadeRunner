# DESIGN - Aging

## Decision

**DECIDED:** PCs **do not die biologically from age** (hidden mechanic). When a protagonist arc closes, narrative focus can **switch to another PC** while the world continues on the same timeline.

## Rationale

- The game spans 30+ canonical years. Realistic metatype lifespans would end Human characters before the timeline does.
- Losing a PC to age after years of play is not fun; the timeline needs to be survivable.
- Narrative focus-switch preserves mortality in fiction while keeping the player engaged.

## Aging presentation

- Characters age visibly in portraits and voice (if implemented) across decades.
- Cosmetic aging cues: hair, scars, cyberware visible wear, voice rasp.
- Mechanical aging is suppressed — stats do not degrade with age.
- In-fiction explanation is deliberately vague ("runners who live this long either get lucky, chromed, awakened, or know someone").

## Protagonist switch

- When a PC's arc reaches a natural conclusion (retirement, apotheosis, death in-run, narrative closure), the player can adopt a new PC.
- The new PC exists in the same world, same save — the previous PC may live on as an NPC or have died.
- The new PC inherits **no** karma/gear, but does inherit:
  - **Contacts-of-contacts** warm introductions.
  - **Reputation shadow** — "the old runner's apprentice" hooks.
  - **Optional legacy assets** — a safehouse, a key heirloom — at a karma cost during chargen.
- New PC chargen re-uses the same karma point-buy system.

## Triggers for switch

- Player-initiated retirement.
- Run-ending permadeath (in lethal-designed runs, or in ironman mode).
- Narrative climax that writes the current PC out.
- A major canonical event the PC does not survive (edge case, rare).

## Lifespan by metatype

- In-fiction only (flavor / NPC lifespans): Human ~70–90, Dwarf ~130, Ork ~40, Troll ~50, Elf effectively very long.
- For the PC, none of these matter mechanically.
- NPCs still age and die; the sim respects metatype lifespans for non-player characters.

## Magic / cyberware life extension (for NPCs)

- Leonization, geneware, initiation bonuses may extend NPC lifespan plausibly in-fiction.
- Useful for keeping key canonical NPCs alive across editions.

## Open questions

- Should the player be *informed* that the PC won't age to death, or is it a hidden mercy?
- How visible is the narrative focus-switch — hard transition, or drift?
- Do legacy assets risk making each successor PC easier than the last?
- Retirement-as-content: can the retired PC be visited as an NPC in subsequent saves?
