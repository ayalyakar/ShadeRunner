# DESIGN - Aging

## Decision

**DECIDED:** Aging is **not mechanically simulated at MVP.** Flavor and narrative only. Metatype lifespan differences (Ork / Troll accelerated aging, Elf longevity) are canonical lore but do not apply stat penalties or content gates in MVP builds.

Post-MVP: reconsider, especially if campaigns stretch many in-game years.

## MVP stance

- PCs and NPCs have canonical ages / lifespans as data in their YAML records, but no runtime aging ticks apply.
- Era drift (2050 → 2064) advances the calendar; PC metatype-specific aging effects not computed.
- Some dialogue / flavor content can reference age and metatype lifespan (e.g. an older Ork contact commenting on the PC outliving their entire gang cohort), but no stat impact.

## Post-MVP possibilities

If aging is later added:

- **Per-metatype lifespan curves** with stat decay at canonical ages.
- **Cyberware / bioware anti-aging options** as an in-world economy layer.
- **Campaign-length gating** so aging only matters on campaigns that span 10+ in-game years.
- **Content reactivity** — NPC contacts age across the campaign; some die of natural causes if long enough.

## Open questions

- Does the player want to feel the passage of time mechanically, or is calendar drift enough?
- Metatype-specific aging rules — do they turn into content (Ork-specific mid-life crisis arc) or just stat drift?
- Anti-aging cyberware / magic — post-MVP content layer, or deliberate omission?
