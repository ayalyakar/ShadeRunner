# DESIGN - Vision

## One-line pitch

A single-player, deeply simulated Shadowrun sandbox where one runner lives, works, and ages through the entire 6th World timeline (2050s → 2080s), in a world that keeps running whether the player acts or not.

## Pillars

1. **Deep simulation first.** The sprawl is a living system — NPCs, factions, economies, tech progression all run autonomously. The player is not the center; they're a participant.
2. **Faithful Shadowrun feel across all editions.** Mechanics cherry-pick from 1E through 6E; lore stays **strictly canonical** (see `Setting/DESIGN - Canon.md`).
3. **The PC is one runner, not a party.** Other runners exist as simulated NPCs; collaboration requires contacts, trust, and negotiation.
4. **All four SR pillars are first-class:** Matrix, Magic, Rigging, Street-sam/combat. Specialization is expected; crossover is expensive.
5. **Genre blend:** immersive sim + life simulation + tactical turn-based + investigation + survival/lifestyle + narrative branching + roguelite consequence.
6. **Player-authored or emergent stories.** Authored main arcs exist, but runs, contacts, and consequences can be fully emergent.

## What this game is

- A long-playtime personal sim where a character can live decades.
- A Shadowrun tabletop experience translated into a videogame with true mechanical depth.
- A toolbox of interlocking systems that produce stories rather than a linear plot.
- A showcase of faithful SR flavor: pink mohawk and black trenchcoat both possible depending on how the world (and player) behaves.

## What this game is not

- Not a party-based tactics game (no BG3/Shadowrun Returns party control).
- Not multiplayer or co-op — strictly single-player.
- Not a linear narrative RPG.
- Not tone-locked: no single dominant tone; tone emerges from circumstance.
- Not MMO, not F2P, not service-driven.

## Audience (of one, but worth naming)

- Shadowrun tabletop veterans who want mechanical depth.
- Immersive-sim / systemic-RPG fans (Dwarf Fortress, Kenshi, Kingdom Come, Disco Elysium).
- Solo-play enjoyers who want a persistent world to return to for years.

## Non-goals

- Mass-market appeal.
- Console or mobile ports.
- Competitive play, leaderboards, achievements-as-retention.

## Success criteria

1. The MVP (Redmond Barrens, street samurai, one run) plays end-to-end and feels like Shadowrun.
2. The sim continues to produce interesting stories with the player's hands off the controls.
3. The developer (me) still wants to play it after five years.
