# DESIGN - Vision

> **Be the runner. Live the world. Die for nuyen.**

## What ShadeRunner is

ShadeRunner is a single-player, forever-in-development simulation of life as a
shadowrunner in the Sixth World. You are chromed, awakened, wired, or jacked
in — in a world that existed before you and does not care whether you live.

It is a personal hobby project. It has no release date, no audience to please,
no commercial intent, and no deadline. Both the design and the implementation
are expected to evolve for as long as the author finds it worthwhile.

## Player fantasy

You are a runner. You take jobs. You make rent. You upgrade your body, or your
deck, or your spells, or your drones. You make enemies. You make friends. You
get old, get crippled, get burned, get ghosted, or get lucky. The world moves
on regardless.

## North stars

These four principles override convenience, scope, and polish when decisions
get hard.

1. **Simulation over abstraction.** Every system simulates rather than
   abstracts. Condition monitors are real wounds. Essence is real nerve damage.
   Nuyen is real supply chains. If a mechanic can be modeled, model it.
2. **The world doesn't revolve around you.** The Sixth World existed before
   the player, exists around them, and continues after them. Canon fires on
   schedule. Factions move on their own. Runners are insects.
3. **Consequence is real.** Karma. Permadeath. Full medical simulation.
   Exploits that actually work. The game never walls the player off from
   consequence in the name of comfort.
4. **Every pillar is a game in itself.** Matrix is not a minigame — it is its
   own full game. Same for Magic, Rigging, Combat, Social, Crafting. Each
   pillar is built to the depth the sourcebooks imply, not to the depth a
   casual player would tolerate.

## Non-goals

These are things ShadeRunner explicitly is not, and will not become.

- **Never commercial or social.** No multiplayer, no leaderboards, no seasons,
  no microtransactions, no always-online. A personal project, forever.
- **No streamlining for mass appeal.** No easy mode, no "press F to hack", no
  tutorial popups past first session. The game does not coddle the player.
- **No player-is-the-chosen-one.** Canon fires whether the player is involved
  or not. No main-character syndrome. No "save the world" arcs bent around the
  player's choices.
- **No competing with AAA visuals.** Art may be stylized, low-fi, mixed-era,
  or placeholder forever. Priority is simulation depth and lore fidelity, not
  pixel fidelity.

## Inspiration

Spiritually closest lineages:

- **Hardcore-sim / milsim:** Escape from Tarkov, Project Zomboid, Silent Storm.
- **Existing Shadowrun videogames:** Shadowrun Returns / Dragonfall / Hong
  Kong, Shadowrun SNES / Genesis, Shadowrun Online experiments.
- **Immersive-sim cyberpunk:** Cyberpunk 2077, Deus Ex (HR / MD), System Shock
  2, E.Y.E: Divine Cybermancy.
- **Emergent-sim / roguelike:** Kenshi, Dwarf Fortress, Caves of Qud, RimWorld.

**Rule of inspiration:** ShadeRunner borrows from *everything* — FromSoftware
games, The Sims, adult / hentai games, board games, tabletop sourcebooks,
films, novels, music. No genre or rating is off-limits as a source. If a
mechanic, feeling, or system fits the Sixth World, it is fair to study and
steal from.

## Source material

The canonical source of truth for the Sixth World is the tabletop material —
Shadowrun First Edition through Sixth World Edition, and every sourcebook,
campaign, adventure, novel, and errata in between. These will be ingested into
the design chapter by chapter, on an ongoing basis, for the life of the
project.

Where editions disagree, design decisions will be made deliberately and
documented, not glossed over.

## Working style

- **Question-driven design.** Every section of the design is opened with a
  round of questions before a single line is written. Assumptions are made
  explicit, not silent.
- **Forever-project mindset.** Nothing in this document is under deadline
  pressure. Depth beats breadth. Quality beats velocity.
- **Design and implementation are co-equal.** A beautiful prototype with no
  design is not progress. A beautiful design with no prototype is not progress
  either. Both move, at their own paces.
