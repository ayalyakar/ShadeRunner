## Downtown

Redmond has no real "downtown" in the corp-tower sense; the district is **post-crash Barrens and decay**. "Downtown" in the sourcebook refers to the strip-mall / commercial remnants concentrated toward the Barrens edge.

Planned hand-built ground for MVP: **Touristville strip** (see MVP Notes below).

## Districts (sub-areas)

- **Redmond Barrens.** Lawless core of the district. MVP starter ground.
- **Touristville.** Shadow-tourism strip: bars, strip clubs, illicit commerce, Johnson meets. The MVP's single hand-crafted sub-zone.
- **Glow City.** Radioactive ruin. Backdrop only in MVP — visible from the skyline but not enterable.

## Government

Legally part of the Seattle Metroplex under UCAS jurisdiction, but functionally lawless in the Barrens. Knight Errant / Lone Star patrol presence is minimal; response times very long; corp security only present when a corp asset is in play.

## Economy

- **Per-capita income:** 6,000¥/year (≈ 500¥/month) is the **anchor** for all lifestyle-tier calibration across the game (see `World/DESIGN - Economy.md`).
- **70% below poverty level.** Squatter and Street lifestyles dominate; Low-tier apartments are an upgrade, not a baseline.
- **22% corporate affiliation** suggests a minority of residents draw corp paychecks or have corp SINs — likely concentrated at the district's edges.

## Crime

- **Gang-dominated.** Canonical gangs active in the Barrens for MVP:
  - **Halloweeners** — fire-themed, chaotic, classic Redmond.
  - **Redmond Hellhounds** — the district's namesake gang, appearing in the extract's history prose.
  - **Spikes** — ork-majority go-gang; fits the 10% ork demographic and biker culture.
- **Organized crime** (Yakuza, Triads, Mafia) present but peripheral in the Barrens; real power lies at district borders.
- **Law enforcement** arrives late if at all; response tiers per `Systems/DESIGN - Combat.md`.

## Hooks (design additions)

- **Tutorial vector.** Touristville fixer calls the PC cold. Low-stakes intro job leads into the district's broader web.
- **Gang triangle.** Halloweeners vs. Hellhounds vs. Spikes is a persistent faction substrate; player choices can tilt it over months/years.
- **Poverty-driven runs.** Exploitation, debt, body-shopping, missing persons — runs that emerge from 70%-below-poverty social conditions.
- **Glow City spectacle.** Visible skyline; occasional runs to the edge; full interior deferred to post-MVP.
